Mashed Potato Cheese Balls

Ingredient Name Quantity
Leftover Mashed Potatoes 2 1/2 cups
Panko Breadcrumbs 1 cup
All Purpose Flour 1 cup
Cheddar Cheese (cut into 18 cubes) 1/2 cup
Eggs 2 pcs.
Vegetable Oil for Frying personal preference
Optional
Salt and Pepper

Steps:

  1. Make the potato balls. Using a tablespoon, scoop out two balls of mashed potatoes, place a cheddar cheese cube on top of one of the balls, and place the other ball on top. Then form the two balls into one larger ball using your hands so that the cheese is contained within. Repeat for the remainder of the mashed potatoes.
  2. Make the dredge. Place the flour, egg, and breadcrumbs in separate bowls or shallow dishes. Mix salt and pepper into the flour*. Have a baking sheet (or large plate) ready for the potato balls.
  3. Take a potato ball, coat first with flour, then egg, and finish was a coat of breadcrumbs before transferring to your baking sheet. Repeat for the rest of the balls.
  4. Fry the potato balls. Using a frying pan or saut√© pan with tall sides, create a shallow fry so that the oil is about 2 inches high in the pan. Heat oil over medium-high heat. Cover a baking sheet (or large plate) with paper towels to absorb excess oil for when the balls are finished frying.
  5. Using a slotted spoon, place 3-4 potato balls into the hot oil (they should not touch). Let one half of the ball fry, getting crispy and brown, before turning the ball over to fry the other half.** Remove the balls and place on paper towels. This should take no longer than 40 seconds for each batch. Repeat for the remaining balls. Sprinkle the balls with sea salt if you like. Serve immediately.
  6. Devour!

Notes:

  • *You can add additional seasoning of your choice to the flour dredge.
  • **You may need to roll the ball around so that all of the ball gets fried.
  • Leftover balls? Reheat at 325 degrees F for 10 minutes from refrigerated.
